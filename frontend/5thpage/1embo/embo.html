Navigating Vascular Malformations

Understanding advanced non-surgical treatment options including Embolization and Sclerotherapy for improved quality of life.

Understanding Vascular Malformations

Vascular malformations are abnormal clusters of blood vessels present at birth or developing over time. They range from cosmetic concerns to serious medical conditions requiring expert care.

Rare & Complex Conditions

Precision-based care by specialized interventional radiologists.

Individualized Treatment

Personalized plans based on type, size, and location.

Early Detection

Early diagnosis improves outcomes and reduces complications.

Embolization

Embolization is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat congenital vascular abnormalities. Using micro-catheters and imaging guidance, abnormal vessels are safely occluded without surgical cuts or stitches.

Sclerotherapy

A cornerstone treatment where a sclerosing agent is injected directly into abnormal vessels, causing them to close and redirect blood flow efficiently.

  • Minimally invasive & outpatient based
  • Suitable for various vascular malformations
  • Faster recovery with minimal discomfort

Advancements in Sclerotherapy

Foam Sclerotherapy

Improved vessel coverage for larger malformations.

Image-Guided Precision

Ultrasound and fluoroscopy ensure safety and accuracy.

Comprehensive Care

Modern vascular care combines embolization, sclerotherapy, laser therapy, and surgery when needed — ensuring optimal patient-focused outcomes.
